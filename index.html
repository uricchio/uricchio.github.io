Computational biology ◆ Evolutionary genetics ◆ Statistical genetics ◆ Quantiative ecology


Thank you for visiting my research page. I am a postdoc in Integrative Biology at UC Berkeley, working with Mike Boots. My research utilizes modern genomic and ecological datasets and statistical inference approaches to infer how evolutionary processes shape phenotypic variation. I am particularly interested in how species adapt (or fail to adapt) to environmental changes, the role of selection in shaping complex phenotypes, and how infectious disease transmission affects biodiversity.

Previously, I was funded through an NIH IRACDA postdoctoral fellowship at Stanford University. As an IRACDA fellow, I co-instructed undergraduate courses in ecology and evolution at SJSU with Scott Shaffer and Leslee Parr. My teaching program focuses on the development of quantitative skills through hypothesis building, data-interpretation, and active learning approaches.
